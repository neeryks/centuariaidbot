1. What is artificial intelligence?


Artificial intelligence, or AI, is the simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems. These processes include learning (the acquisition of information and skills), reasoning (using the information to reach conclusions), and self-correction. AI applications can analyze large amounts of data and recognize patterns to make decisions in real time.

AI has been around for decades, but it has only recently become powerful enough to be used in commercial applications. In the past, AI was used mainly for research purposes or in specialized applications such as games or finance. However, in recent years AI has become more accessible and is being used in a wider range of applications, such as image recognition, natural language processing, and translation.

AI is used to make decisions in a number of different ways. Some AI applications use decision trees, which are a series of questions that help the AI system determine the best course of action. Other AI applications use artificial neural networks, which are modeled after the brain and can learn by example.

AI is often used to make decisions in complex situations where it is difficult for humans to process all the information. For example, AI can be used to make decisions about where to send a drone to deliver a package or to identify cancerous cells in a medical image.

AI is also being used to create intelligent assistants that can help humans with tasks such as scheduling appointments or ordering food. These assistants use natural language processing to understand the user’s needs and then take the appropriate action.

AI is also being used to create autonomous vehicles. These vehicles use a variety of sensors to detect their surroundings and then make decisions about how to navigate the environment.
