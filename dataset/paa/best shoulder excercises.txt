best shoulder excercises

1. Dumbbell shoulder press


The dumbbell shoulder press is an effective exercise for targeting the shoulders. It can be performed either seated or standing.

To perform the exercise, hold a dumbbell in each hand and raise them to shoulder height, with your palms facing forward. Then, press the weights overhead until your arms are fully extended. Reverse the motion and lower the weights back to the starting position.

The dumbbell shoulder press can be performed with a variety of weight levels, depending on your fitness level and experience. Start with a light weight and gradually increase the weight as you become stronger.
2. Seated or standing cable shoulder press


The seated or standing cable shoulder press is a great exercise for working your shoulder muscles. It can be done with either a standing or seated position.

To do the seated version, sit with your back against a bench, and hold the cable handles with your hands shoulder-width apart. Press the handles forward until your arms are fully extended. Reverse the motion to return to the starting position.

To do the standing version, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold the cable handles with your hands shoulder-width apart. Press the handles forward until your arms are fully extended. Reverse the motion to return to the starting position.
3. Arnold press


The Arnold press is a shoulder exercise that is named after legendary bodybuilder and actor Arnold Schwarzenegger. This exercise is a variation of the shoulder press that targets the anterior deltoids and the triceps.

To perform the Arnold press, you will need a weight bench, weight plates, and a barbell. Sit on the bench with your feet flat on the floor and shoulder-width apart. Hold the barbell with your hands shoulder-width apart and your palms facing your thighs.

Press the barbell overhead until your arms are fully extended. Keep your elbows close to your head and your shoulder blades retracted. Reverse the motion and lower the barbell to the starting position.
4. Behind the neck press


The behind-the-neck press is an exercise used to target the muscles of the shoulder. It is a variation of the shoulder press that is performed by standing with the barbell behind the head, rather than in front of the head. This exercise is not recommended for beginners, as it can be difficult to maintain proper form and can lead to injury. 

The behind-the-neck press can be performed with a barbell or with dumbbells. To begin, stand with the feet hip-width apart and hold the weight(s) with an overhand grip, palms facing forward. Bend the elbows and lower the weight(s) down to the shoulder blades. Keep the elbows close to the body and press the weight(s) back up to the starting position.
5. Upright row


The upright row is a great exercise for targeting the muscles of the back and shoulders. It can be performed with a barbell, dumbbells, or cables.

To perform the upright row, stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold a weight in each hand. Bend your elbows and pull the weights up towards your chest, keeping your shoulders down and your back straight. Pause for a moment, then slowly lower the weights back to the starting position.
6. Lateral raise


The lateral raise is a move that works the muscles of the shoulder. It is a good move to do if you are looking to build strength and definition in the shoulders.

To do the lateral raise, you will need to stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold a weight in each hand. Bend your elbows and bring your hands to your sides.

Then, lift your hands out to the sides until your arms are parallel to the ground. Hold for a few seconds, and then lower them back to your sides.
7. Front raise


The front raise is a simple isolation exercise that targets the front deltoids. This exercise can be performed with a cable machine, dumbbells, or barbell.

To perform the front raise, stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold a weight in each hand with your palms facing your thighs.

Keeping your core engaged, slowly lift your arms in front of you until your hands are shoulder height. Pause for a moment, then slowly lower your arms back to the starting position.
8. Bent over lateral raise


The bent over lateral raise is a great exercise for developing the lateral deltoid muscles. This exercise can be performed with either free weights or a cable machine.

To perform the bent over lateral raise with free weights, stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold a weight in each hand with your palms facing your thighs. Bend your knees and hinge forward at the waist, keeping your back flat. Raise your arms out to the sides until they are parallel to the floor. Pause briefly and then lower the weights back to the starting position.

To perform the bent over lateral raise with a cable machine, stand with the cable machine at your side. Grab the cable with your right hand and step back a few feet so there is tension on the cable. Bend your knees and hinge forward at the waist, keeping your back flat. Raise your arm out to the side until it is parallel to the floor. 9. Reverse fly


The reverse fly is a great exercise for targeting the muscles in the upper back. It also works the shoulder muscles and the core muscles.

To do the reverse fly, you will need a pair of dumbbells.

Hold the dumbbells with your hands shoulder-width apart and your palms facing your thighs.

Keeping your back straight, bend your knees slightly and hinge forward at the hips.

Raise the dumbbells out to the sides, keeping your arms straight.

Pinch your shoulder blades together as you raise the weights.

Lower the weights back to the starting position.

Repeat the exercise 10-12 times.
